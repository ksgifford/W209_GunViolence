Gun violence is present throughout the developed world.


The portion of people dying from gun injuries varies significantly by country.


The OECD (Organization for Economic Co-Operation and Development) is a group of 36 countries with high-income economies, regarded as very developed countries.


Interact with the chart below to see what percentage of deaths are related to guns and other causes in each OECD country.


*Note: All other causes of deaths include the following but are not limited to: poisonings, exposure to mechanical forces, animal contact, unintentional suffocation, adverse effects of medical treatment, and more.

Spatial patterns of gun violence differ widely from state to state over time.


Gun-realted suicides follow a spatial pattern distinct from gun-related murders.


Click on a state to explore county-level data and use the slider to see how death rates have changed over time.
