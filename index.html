An examination of gun violence trends is incomplete without considering who perpetrates violence with firearms.

Perpetrators fall into 3 broad categories:

  • 1. Those who commit individual murders (majority using firearms)
  • 2. Those who use firearms in mass shootings
  • 3. Those who commit suicide with firearms
While the motivations of those who do harm with guns is often hotly debated, the data presented here indicates distinct differences in lifestage for these three categories.

People are most likely to commit murders when they are young, with one quarter of all murders committed in the early 20s, and more than half between the ages of 17 to 29.

Mass shooters skew slightly older, with 50% of events occurring when perpetrators are 25 to 39 years old.

Suicide by firearms spans a very broad age range, but is most pronounced for people in their 50s.
